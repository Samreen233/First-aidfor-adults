Providing psychological first aid(continued)

Learning how to provide someone in distress with psychological first aid can help the person deal with the trauma they may have experienced. When we experience frightening or distressing events such as accidents, injuries, fires, or disasters, we react with our bodies, thoughts, and feelings. Being aware of different signs of distress will help you to recognize when someone may need psychological first aid. Typical signs of distress can be broadly separated into four categories:

  • Physical Reactions: May include trembling, shaking, and vomiting
  • Cognitive Reactions: May include confusion, losing sense of place and time, and disorientation
  • Emotional Reactions: May include anger, anxiety, greif, guilt, and shame
  • Behavioral Reactions: Examples include agresive behavior and irritability
