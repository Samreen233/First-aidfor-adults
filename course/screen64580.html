Adapted ABC's of first aid

A:Airway

Lay the casualty on their back but do not tilt the casualty’s head back to open their airway. Do not attempt to open their mouth in order to reduce the risk of the virus being spread in the air (aerosolization)

B:Breathing

Do not lean over the casualty’s face. Do not put your ear/cheek near the child’s mouth or nose to check for breathing, instead watch to see if the child’s stomach and chest rise and fall.

C:Circulation

There are no adaptations for checking circulation. Follow the usual steps: Are there any signs of external bleeding? Are they bleeding under their clothes? Is the person's skin blue, pink, or pale? Are their hands and feet warm or cold?
