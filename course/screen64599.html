Recognizing the signs of cardiac arrest

The first step to assess if someone is in cardiac arrest is to look for signs that the person is breathing normally. As shown in the video, this can be done by tilting the person’s head back and lifting their chin to open the airway. You should lower your cheek to their mouth to feel for any breathing, whilst also watching their chest to see if there is any movement from the person’s lungs. If there are no signs of breathing, you will need to provide chest compressions as a minimum and rescue breaths if you can.
