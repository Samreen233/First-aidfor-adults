Complications associated with bleeding

Sometimes, there are additional concerns when a person is bleeding. Many of these are unlikely to happen, but being prepared for any issue is important for all first aiders. Consider the following:

  • An immediate complication associated with bleeding is shock. Signs of shock in an adult can include paleness, cold and clammy skin, confusion, difficulty breathing and, in very extreme circumstances, the person could become unresponsive. Help the person to lay down and call for medical help immediately if you see any of these signs.
