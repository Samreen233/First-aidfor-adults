Key points on CPR
  • To assess if a person is breathing, use your cheek to feel for any breath and look to see if their chest is rising and falling.
  • If there is no response from the person and they are not breathing normally, start CPR immediately. If an AED is available, use this immediately, giving CPR while the AED is prepared.
  • Start CPR by providing 30 chest compressions. The chest should be compressed to a depth of approximately 5 - 6cm. You should allow the chest to fully re-expand between compressions.
  • If you are willing and able, follow each round of 30 chest compressions with two rescue breaths.
  • If you cannot simultaneously call for help, give CPR for at least one minute before calling for help.
  • Keep the time between breaths and compressions to a minimum and avoid leaning on the chest with your full weight between compressions.
  • Chest compressions should be given at a continuous rate of between 100 and 120 compressions per minute.
  • Continue the cycle of 30 chest compressions and two rescue breaths until emergency help arrives or until the person shows signs of recovery.
