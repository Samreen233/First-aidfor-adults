Reducing the risk of injury

Many people are worried that they could hurt the person if they push too hard or in the incorrect position. However, by adapting the force with which you deliver chest compressions to the size of the person, the risk of further injury can be minimized. Those delivering first aid should aim to compress the chest by about 5 - 6cm. This will minimize the risk of harm whilst ensuring sufficient force for correct compressions. Click on the image below to learn simple steps to reduce the chance of injury when delivering chest compressions.

  • It is vital when providing chest compressions that you avoid learning on the person's chest with your body weight between chest compressions. Try to allow the chest wall to fully expand after each compression.
  • Chest compressions should be performed on the center of the person's chest (i.e. the lower part of the breast bone).
  • For children and infants, the technique is different. Learn more by taking the First Aid for Babies and Children course.
