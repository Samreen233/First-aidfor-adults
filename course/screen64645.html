When is a stroke not a stroke?

In some cases, stroke-like symptoms may be mild and only last a short duration. These are known as transient ischemic attacks (don’t worry, you don’t have to remember that!). Transient ischemic attacks are warning or mini strokes that results in no lasting brain injury. The short duration of the symptoms is the key indication that this may not a stroke. Nevertheless, it is important that the person seeks medical help as early treatment to reduce the risk of a major stroke could be life-saving. As a first aider, help the person to call emergency medical services attention immediately.
