Signs of choking include
  • Coughing, either forcefully or weakly
  • Making high-pitched noises while inhaling, especially noisy breathing or displaying an inability to cough, speak, cry or breathe
  • Showing signs of panic including clutching the throat with one or both hands
  • Bluish skin color and/ or losing consciousness if the blockage is not removed

Do no harm

The faster you can identify that a person is choking and needs your help, the quicker you can provide potentially life-saving first aid. However, it is important to learn to recognize the signs of choking and when to act. Remember that you should only interfere if the person is unable to breathe and the situation is worsening.
