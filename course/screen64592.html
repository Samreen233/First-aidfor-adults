The recovery position for adults

The recovery position is the safest action to take with an injured person that is unresponsive, but still breathing normally. Study the techniques shown in the video carefully.

  • The three key points to notice are the hand placement, the use of the bent leg to roll the person onto their side, and the placement of the bent leg after rolling the person onto their side.
  • If you are alone and unable to physically roll the injured person onto their side, ask for help. It is important that the person is put into the position carefully. If the casualty is pregnant, try to roll them onto their left side in the recovery position.
  • After you have successfully placed the person into the recovery position, call for help. Continually monitor the person, especially their breathing, until help arrives. If they suddenly stop breathing normally, you will need to act. We will learn how to help someone who is unresponsive and not breathing normally in Module 2.
