Welcome

According to the World Health Organization (WHO), 5.8 million people die as a result of injury every year, accounting for more deaths than malaria, tuberculosis (TB), and HIV/AIDS combined (WHO, 2011). Cardiovascular disease is the second leading cause of death and the third leading cause of disability in adults around the world (WHO, 2016). Road traffic accidents kill more people aged 5 to 29 than any other cause of death (WHO, 2019) and burns, falls, and violence claim thousands of lives and disable thousands more every single day. These numbers are scary. But, by equipping yourself with basic first aid knowledge and skills, you can save lives at the scene of an accident or emergency. And that, at the Red Cross and Red Crescent, is what we're all about-- saving lives and changing minds. This course on First Aid for Adults will introduce you to the key concepts of first aid and empower you to take action to support someone in need.
