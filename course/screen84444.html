Providing psychological first aid(continued)

In order to provide psychological first aid effectively, it is recommended that you use the three psychological first aid action points: Look, Listen, and Link. 

Look

Prioritize: Who needs your help the most? Are there many people displaying signs of distress and who do you feel needs your help most urgently?

Emotional reactions: Is anyone so distressed that you feel they need professional help that is beyond the scope of your capabilities in psychological first aid?

Behavioral reactions: Is someone acting in a way that is a danger to themselves or those around them? What can you say or do to keep yourself and those around you safe?

Listen

Approach the person who needs help: Be calm, respectful, and act in a culturally appropriate way. Introduce yourself and ask the person if they need help, but do not impose help if they do not want it.

Show that you are paying attention: Listen actively through your body language and attitude. Ask about their needs and concerns. Try to help the affected person(s) identify what help they need. Do not ask them to go into detail about what has happened or how they feel - focus on what they need. Gather as much information as you can to help you link them to the help that they need.

Show that you accept their feelings: Do not judge the personâ€™s reactions or feelings, even if they are different from how you think you would react.

Calm the person: Help the person feel calm and supported, by remaining calm yourself, speaking in a soft voice and supporting the person to breathe and maintain focus.

Link

Help people address basic needs:Does the person need a warm blanket or some water to drink?

Connect: Help the injured person to connect with loved ones and other social support.

Provide information: Provide factual information about the event and where to get further help to access emergency services, for example.
