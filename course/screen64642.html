Recognizing the symptoms of a stroke

A stroke can be recognized using the acronym FAST. Click on the image below to learn more.

    F: Face drooping

  1. Does one side of the face droop or is it numb? Ask the person to smile. Is the person's smile uneven or lopsided?

    2. A: Arm Weakness

  2. Is one arm weak or numb? Ask the person to raise both arms. Does one arm fall further downward than the other?

    3. S: Speech difficulty

  3. Is their speech slurred, or are they completely unable to speak? Ask the person to repeat a simple sentence. Are they able to repeat it correctly?

    4. T: Time to call for help

  4. If someone shows these symptoms, call emergency services immediately or get them to a medical facility as quickly and safely as possible.
