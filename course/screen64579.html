Providing first aid to a person with symptoms of COVID-19

When providing first-aid to a person who is presenting symptoms that could suggest an infectious disease like COVID-19, the first aiders must take measures to protect themselves and those nearby:

  • Wash your hands before any intervention.
  • Keep as much distance between yourself and the person presenting symptoms. Do touch the person, especially if they are not wearing a mask. If you must be close to the person, wear a mask.
  • Ask the casualty to wear a mask and isolate themselves in a separate room if possible. If the mask affects the person’s ventilation, it should be removed.
  • Ask the sick person’s friends/family and contacts to respect distancing measures and hygiene practices.
  • Do not touch your eyes, nose, mouth or face or those of the causality.
  • At the end of the intervention, wash your hands with soap and water. Dry them with a clean towel or tissue. Ask the casualty to do the same. If running water is not available, you can use hand sanitiser or ash to disinfect your hands.

Remember, you must wash your hands both BEFORE and AFTER providing first aid.
