Automated external defibrillator (AED)

What is an AED?

An AED is a device used to deliver an electric shock to a person experiencing a cardiac arrest. This helps the heart to re-establish an effective rhythm. You may have seen medical professionals use them in real life or on TV. They are also often found in public spaces to facilitate first aid. AEDs are designed to be used by anyone. Instructions on how to use the AED safely and effectively are provided with the equipment. If you are in a situation where someone is not responding and has stopped breathing, begin CPR immediately and always ask if an AED is available. Remember, they are specifically designed to be used by someone without any medical training. If an AED is available, always continue to provide CPR whilst the AED is being prepared for use. In many situations, an AED may not be available, therefore, it should not be relied upon. Good CPR technique and the prompt arrival of professional medical help is vital to ensure survival following cardiac arrest.
